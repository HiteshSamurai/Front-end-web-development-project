Education

Education is the kindling of a flame, not the filling of a vessel.

  • I attendedS.A.V. High School, a relatively unknown institution in the erstwhile sleepy suburb of a growing city. Though I had the best learning experience there, it is a common trend accross India that sports and cocurricular activities are not much encouraged. Even then, we did have competitions within our school and I excelled inelocution, art and prompt writing. My best achievement was getting an all Telangana 10th rank in CLB Science Talent Search Examination, 2017. This achievement inspired me to take up coaching for JEE.

  • After completing my 10th grade, I attended Little Flower Junior College, Uppal,which is one of the best junior colleges in our city. StudyingMPC (Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry)under the guidance of well versed lecturers was a blessing. Another important aspect of this college is the strict discipline as mandated by missionary schools, which has been of great utility to me in my life. I also made great friends along the way who are doing well.

  • After a tiresome 12-month-long struggle, I was successful in cracking the toughest exam in India - the JEE-ADVANCED. I was able to secure mechanical engineering programme at IIT Ropar. I really like the experience I am having at IIT Ropar.

    First thing is that you can find a dedicated motivating tribe of people with great aspirations like you. Second thing is that a multiethnic community with people coming from spectrum of views mean that students have a higher chance of embracing progressive thinking and tolerance. Third is the university culture that encourages a myraid of activities other than studying like research, critical thinking, cocurricular activities like sport, art and music, taking up the roles of POR in your favourite clubs and leadership roles.
